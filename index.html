Simon's Seven: My Favourite Classic Bicycles

1984 Bianchi Specialissima

This classic road bike in the iconic Bianchi "celeste" blue-green always turns heads. The colour is an homage to the beautiful skies of Milan, although rumour has it this was originally from surplus military paint.

1991 Bridgestone RB-T

This cult favourite from Grant Peterson's days at Bridgestone was a light touring bike made in Japan that showed Peterson's independant and forward thinking mindset, before he broke out on his own and founded the boutique brand Rivendell.

1987 Miyata 1000 LT

Considered by many to be the best off-the-shelf touring bike ever made, this bike offers ample tire clearance, plenty of braze-ons, and a generous wheelbase. Built using Miyata's proprietary splined, triple-butted tubing, these are the holy grail of vintage touring bikes.

1992 Specialized Hardrock

Incredibly versatile, these early 90s Hardrocks have seen a huge resurgence and seemed destined to be timeless classics. Those fonts and paint jobs!

1982 Rocky Mountain Sherpa

Made in the Toyo factory in Japan, this lugged chromoly frame was the first production bike from Rocky Mountain, back when it was a couple guys in Vancouver trying to figure out how to design a bike with clearance for wide tires.

1998 Marinoni Turismo

Handmade with Columbus tubing, this Canadian classic blends speed with loaded touring capabilities.

1982 Specialized Sequoia

One of the very first offerings from Specialized, this "sports tourer" all rouder featured Tange tubing and a great ride quality.
